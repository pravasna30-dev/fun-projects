# Aditya Hridayam — Companion Guide for Recording & YouTube Chapters

Source text: `aditya_english.pdf` (transliteration only). English meanings below are
cross-checked against published translations (greenmesg.org, hindu-blog.com,
stotranidhi.com) — see notes at the end for anything genuinely disputed among
scholars.

**Total chapters: 34** — Invocation, Verses 1–30, the closing narrative verse,
the Dhyana verse, and the final salutation verse. (The bracketed line "Iti
Aarshe Srimad Ramaayane..." is a scribal colophon marking the end of the
Ramayana chapter — it's traditionally read as a note, not chanted, so it's
left out of the chapter list. Say so if you'd rather include it as a spoken
line.)

Each entry below = one YouTube chapter = one audio clip when you record.

---

## Ch. 1 — Invocation

**Sanskrit**
Namah savitre jagadeka chakshushe
jagatprasooti sthiti naasha hetave
Trayee mayaaya trigunaatma dhaarine
virinchi naaraayana shankaraatmane

**Meaning**
Salutations to Savitṛ (the Sun), the single eye of the whole universe, the
cause of its creation, sustenance, and dissolution — embodiment of the three
Vedas and the three guṇas, who is himself Brahmā, Nārāyaṇa, and Śaṅkara.

---

## Ch. 2 — Verse 1

**Sanskrit**
Tato yuddha parishraantam samare chintayaa sthitam
Raavanam chaagrato drishtvaa yuddhaaya samupasthitam

**Meaning**
Then (Agastya) saw Rāma standing on the battlefield, exhausted from fighting
and lost in thought, with Rāvaṇa in front of him, ready for battle.

---

## Ch. 3 — Verse 2

**Sanskrit**
Daivataishcha samaagamya drashtu mabhyaagato ranam
Upaagamyaabraveed ramam agastyo bhagavaan rishih

**Meaning**
Having come together with the gods to witness the battle, the venerable sage
Agastya approached and said to Rāma:

---

## Ch. 4 — Verse 3

**Sanskrit**
Rama Rama mahaa baaho shrunu guhyam sanaatanam
Yena sarvaanareen vatsa samare vijayishyasi

**Meaning**
"O Rāma, Rāma, mighty-armed one, listen to this eternal secret, by which, my
child, you will defeat all your enemies in this battle."

---

## Ch. 5 — Verse 4

**Sanskrit**
Aaditya Hridayam punyam sarva shatru vinaashanam
Jayaavaham japennityam akshayyam paramam shivam

**Meaning**
"The sacred Aditya Hridayam, destroyer of all enemies and bringer of
victory — one who recites it daily attains the imperishable, supreme
auspiciousness."

---

## Ch. 6 — Verse 5

**Sanskrit**
Sarva mangala maangalyam sarva paapa pranaashanam
Chintaa shoka prashamanam ayur vardhanamuttamam

**Meaning**
It is the most auspicious of all auspicious things, destroyer of all sins,
pacifier of anxiety and grief, and the best enhancer of life span.

---

## Ch. 7 — Verse 6

**Sanskrit**
Rashmi mantam samudyantam devaasura namaskritam
Poojayasva vivasvantam bhaaskaram bhuvaneshvaram

**Meaning**
Worship Vivasvān (the Sun), full of rays, rising and spreading light equally
for all, saluted by both gods and demons — the illuminator, lord of the
worlds.

---

## Ch. 8 — Verse 7

**Sanskrit**
Sarva devaatmako hyesha tejasvee rashmi bhaavanah
Esha devaasura ganaan lokaan paati gabhastibhih

**Meaning**
He carries the essence/soul of all the gods, radiant and full of energy which
he manifests through his rays; with those rays he protects the worlds — gods,
demons, and all beings.

---

## Ch. 9 — Verse 8

**Sanskrit**
Esha brahmaa cha vishnuscha shivah skandah prajaapatih
Mahendro dhanadah kaalo yamassomo hyappam patih

**Meaning**
He is Brahmā, Viṣṇu, Śiva, Skanda, and Prajāpati; he is Indra, Kubera (lord of
wealth), Kāla (time), Yama, Soma (moon), and Varuṇa (lord of the waters).

---

## Ch. 10 — Verse 9

**Sanskrit**
Pitaro vasavassaadhyaa hyashvinou maruto manuh
Vaayur vahnih prajaah praana ritukartaa prabhaakarah

**Meaning**
He is the Pitṛs (ancestors), the Vasus, the Sādhyas, the two Aśvins, the
Maruts, and Manu; he is Vāyu (wind), Vahni (fire), all beings, the life-force,
the regulator of the seasons, and the maker of light.

---

## Ch. 11 — Verse 10

**Sanskrit**
Aadityassavitaa sooryah khagah pooshaa gabhastimaan
Suvarna sadrisho bhaanur hiranyaretaa divaakarah

**Meaning**
He is Āditya, Savitṛ, Sūrya, Khaga (sky-goer), Pūṣā, full of rays; golden in
hue, radiant (Bhānu), of golden seed (Hiraṇyaretas), maker of the day
(Divākara).

---

## Ch. 12 — Verse 11

**Sanskrit**
Haridashwah sahasraarchih sapta saptir mareechimaan
Timironmathanah shambhuh stvashtaa maartanda amshumaan

**Meaning**
He has tawny horses, a thousand rays, seven horses, and is full of light; he
churns away darkness, is Śambhu, Tvaṣṭā, Mārtaṇḍa, the possessor of rays.

---

## Ch. 13 — Verse 12

**Sanskrit**
Hiranya garbhah shishirah tapano bhaaskaro ravih
Agni garbhoditeh putrah shankhah shishira naashanah

**Meaning**
He is Hiraṇyagarbha (golden-wombed), Śiśira, Tapana (the warmer), Bhāskara,
Ravi; born of Agni's womb, son of Aditi; Śaṅkha; destroyer of cold.

---

## Ch. 14 — Verse 13

**Sanskrit**
Vyoma naathah stamo bhedee rig yajussaama paaragah
Ghana vrishti rapaam mitro vindhya veethee plavagamah

**Meaning**
He is lord of the sky, breaker of darkness, master of the Ṛg, Yajur, and Sāma
Vedas; friend of the rain-bearing clouds, and — the traditionally debated
phrase — one who swiftly courses along the path south of the Vindhya
mountains, like a monkey leaping (during the sun's southward journey,
dakṣiṇāyana).

---

## Ch. 15 — Verse 14

**Sanskrit**
Aatapee mandalee mrityuh pingalah sarva taapanah
Kavir vishwo mahaa tejaa raktassarva bhavod bhavah

**Meaning**
He gives heat, has a great orb, is (an aspect of) death itself, reddish-brown
in color, scorcher of all; the wise one, the universe itself, of great
splendor, red-hued, the origin of all existence.

---

## Ch. 16 — Verse 15

**Sanskrit**
Nakshatra graha taaraanaam adhipo vishva bhaavanah
Tejasaamapi tejasvee dvaadashaatman namostute

**Meaning**
He is the presiding lord of the constellations, planets, and stars, the
creator/nurturer of the universe, the most radiant among the radiant, the one
with twelve forms — salutations to you!

---

## Ch. 17 — Verse 16

**Sanskrit**
Namah poorvaaya giraye paschimaayaadraye namah
Jyotirganaanaam pataye dinaadhipataye namah

**Meaning**
Salutations to him at the eastern mountain (where he rises), salutations to
him at the western mountain (where he sets); salutations to the lord of the
hosts of luminaries, the lord of the day.

---

## Ch. 18 — Verse 17

**Sanskrit**
Jayaaya jaya bhadraaya haryashvaaya namo namah
Namo namah sahasraamsho aadityaaya namo namah

**Meaning**
Salutations again and again to him who is Victory itself, who brings
auspicious victory, whose horses are tawny; salutations again and again to
the thousand-rayed Āditya.

---

## Ch. 19 — Verse 18

**Sanskrit**
Nama ugraaya veeraaya saarangaaya namo namah
Namah padma prabodhaaya maartaandaaya namo namah

**Meaning**
Salutations to the fierce and valiant one, of variegated/swift form;
salutations again and again to the one who awakens the lotus, Mārtaṇḍa.

---

## Ch. 20 — Verse 19

**Sanskrit**
Brahmeshaanaachyuteshaaya sooryaayaaditya varchase
Bhaasvate sarva bhakshaaya roudraaya vapushe namah

**Meaning**
Salutations to him who is the lord of Brahmā, Īśāna (Śiva), and Acyuta
(Viṣṇu); to Sūrya of the splendor of Āditya, the radiant one who consumes/
sustains all, of fierce (Rudra-like) form.

---

## Ch. 21 — Verse 20

**Sanskrit**
Tamoghnaaya himaghnaaya shatrughnaaya mitaatmane
Kritaghaghnaaya devaaya jyotishaam pataye namah

**Meaning**
Salutations to the destroyer of darkness, destroyer of cold, destroyer of
enemies, of measured/disciplined soul; destroyer of the sins of the
ungrateful, the god who is lord of all luminaries.

---

## Ch. 22 — Verse 21

**Sanskrit**
Tapta chaameekaraabhaaya vahnaye vishwa karmane
Namastamobhi nighnaaya ruchaye loka saakshine

**Meaning**
Salutations to the one who shines like molten gold, the fire that powers all
activity in the universe, the destroyer of darkness, the brilliance that
witnesses the whole world.

---

## Ch. 23 — Verse 22

**Sanskrit**
Naashayatyesha vai bhootam tadeva srijati prabhuh
Paayatyesha tapatyesha varshatyesha gabhastibhih

**Meaning**
He indeed is the lord who destroys beings, and the very same one creates
them; he protects, he gives heat, and he sends rain, all through his rays.

---

## Ch. 24 — Verse 23

**Sanskrit**
Esha supteshu jaagarti bhooteshu pari nishthitah
Esha chaivaagni hotrancha phalam chivaagni hotrinaam

**Meaning**
He remains awake even while all beings sleep, abiding within those very
beings; he is the fire-sacrifice itself, and the fruit of that sacrifice for
those who perform it.

---

## Ch. 25 — Verse 24

**Sanskrit**
Vedaashcha kratavashchaiva kratoonaam phalameva cha
Yaani krityaani lokeshu sarva esha ravih prabhuh

**Meaning**
He is the Vedas, the rituals, and the fruit of those rituals; whatever
actions are performed in the worlds, Ravi the lord is behind all of it.

---

## Ch. 26 — Verse 25

**Sanskrit**
Enam aapatsu krichchreshu kaantaareshu bhayeshu cha
Keertayan purushah kashchin naavaseedati raaghava

**Meaning**
O Rāghava, any person who praises him in calamities, hardships, forests, or
moments of fear never sinks into despair.

---

## Ch. 27 — Verse 26

**Sanskrit**
Poojayasvaina mekaagro deva devam jagatpatim
Etat trigunitam japtvaa yuddheshu vijayishyasi

**Meaning**
Worship him with one-pointed devotion, the god of gods, lord of the universe;
having recited this three times, you will be victorious in battle.

---

## Ch. 28 — Verse 27

**Sanskrit**
Asmin kshane mahaa baaho raavanam tvam vadhishyasi
Evamuktvaa tadaagastyo jagaama cha yathaagatam

**Meaning**
"This very moment, O mighty-armed one, you will slay Rāvaṇa." Having said
this, sage Agastya departed the way he had come.

---

## Ch. 29 — Verse 28

**Sanskrit**
Etachchrutvaa mahaa tejaa nashta shoko bhavattadaa
Dhaarayaamaasa supreeto raghavah prayataatmavaan

**Meaning**
Hearing this, Rāma of great splendor became free of all sorrow; delighted and
composed, Rāghava took the teaching to heart.

---

## Ch. 30 — Verse 29

**Sanskrit**
Aadityam prekshya japtvaa tu param harshamavaaptavaan
Triraachamya shuchir bhootvaa dhanu raadaaya veeryavaan

**Meaning**
Gazing at the sun and reciting the hymn, he felt supreme joy; sipping water
three times to purify himself, the valiant one took up his bow.

---

## Ch. 31 — Verse 30

**Sanskrit**
Raavanam prekshya hirshtaatmaa yuddhaaya samupaagamat
Sarva yatnena mahataa vadhe tasya dhrito bhavat

**Meaning**
Seeing Rāvaṇa, glad at heart, he advanced to battle, resolved with all his
strength and effort to slay him.

---

## Ch. 32 — Closing narrative verse

**Sanskrit**
Atha ravi ravadannirikshya raamam
mudita manaah paramam prahrishyamaanah
Nishi chara pati samkshayam viditvaa
suragana madhyagato vachastvareti

**Meaning**
Then the Sun, looking at Rāma, his heart full of joy and delight, knowing
that the destruction of the lord of the night-wandering demons was at hand,
spoke swiftly from amidst the gathered gods (urging Rāma on).

*(The bracketed colophon "Iti Aarshe Srimad Ramaayane yuddha kaande Aaditya
Hridaya Stotram naama saptottara shata tamah sargah samaaptah" follows here in
the text — "Thus ends the Aditya Hridaya Stotra, the 107th chapter of the
Yuddha Kanda of the venerable Ramayana." It's a scribal end-note, not
traditionally chanted.)*

---

## Ch. 33 — Dhyana verse

**Sanskrit**
Dhyeyassadaa savitr mandala madhya vartee
Naaraayanah sarasijaasana sannivishtah
Keyooravaan makara kundalavaan kireetee
Haaree hiranmaya vapur dhrita shankha chakrah

**Meaning**
One should always meditate on Nārāyaṇa dwelling at the center of the sun's
orb, seated on a lotus-throne, adorned with armlets, shark-shaped earrings,
and a crown — golden-bodied, holding the conch and discus.

---

## Ch. 34 — Final salutation

**Sanskrit**
Mitra ravi soorya bhaanu khaga poosha
Hiranyagarbha mareechi aaditya
savitrarka bhaaskarebhyo namah

**Meaning**
Salutations to Mitra, Ravi, Sūrya, Bhānu, Khaga, Pūṣā, Hiraṇyagarbha, Marīci,
Āditya, Savitṛ, Arka, and Bhāskara — the twelve names/forms of the Sun.

# Notes / open questions

1. **Verse 13** ("Vindhya veethee plavagamah") has a genuinely disputed
   reading among traditional commentators — some read it as the sun's
   southward path near the Vindhyas, others tie "plavaga" (monkey) to a
   different symbolic sense. The rendering above reflects the most common
   published interpretation.
2. Audio source is still open — see the earlier discussion about not reusing
   the reference YouTube video's recording without a confirmed Creative
   Commons license or the uploader's written permission.
